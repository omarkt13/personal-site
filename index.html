about writing photography consulting

hi, i'm omar.

i work on how capital moves development, specifically the gap between what gets promised and what actually happens on the ground.

i started in consulting, coordinating large-scale government reform. one project involved aligning ten government entities on a $200b+ budget process. another lifted a national transparency index by 18 points. i also worked the private capital side of that world, clearing a major regulatory merger, finding value in overlooked operational details, screening energy investments for institutional deployment.

in 2022 i left to build something of my own: a fintech focused on financial planning. i learned what it takes to go from an idea to a working product, and what breaks when you're the one accountable for all of it.

i'm now moving into a role focused on cross-sector development at a sovereign wealth fund. the question i'm sitting with is why capital deployed for development so often fails to shift outcomes, and what it would take for a fund with mandate across multiple sectors to coordinate capital differently than a single investor could.

based between geneva and riyadh. i'm starting to write about this properly, mostly because i think the current language around impact investing doesn't hold up to scrutiny.

outside of work: ski touring, scuba diving, sailing, and an unreasonable amount of care put into espresso.
